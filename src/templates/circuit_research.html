The Science Behind Circuit

Our approach is rooted in decades of behavioral psychology research on addiction, connection, and well-being.

The Rat Park Experiment

In 1978, psychologist Bruce Alexander conducted a groundbreaking experiment that challenged everything we thought we knew about addiction.

Previous studies had shown that isolated rats would compulsively consume morphine-laced water until they died. But Alexander asked: what if the problem wasn't the drug - what if it was the isolation?

He created "Rat Park" - a spacious, enriched environment where rats could socialize, play, and mate. The results were stunning: rats in Rat Park showed almost no interest in the drugged water.

The Digital Cage

Today's social media platforms have become the opposite of Rat Park:

  • Shallow connections: Likes and comments trigger dopamine but don't satisfy our need for genuine bonding
  • Social comparison: Curated feeds create anxiety and inadequacy, not belonging
  • Variable rewards: Infinite scroll mimics slot machine psychology
  • Attention extraction: Every feature maximizes time-on-app, not well-being
